Why You Can’t Watch Manifest Season 2 and 3 on Netflix (Yet)

Manifest is a supernatural drama series that follows the passengers and crew of a plane that mysteriously reappears after being presumed dead for five years.

The show has been a huge hit on Netflix, where it ranks among the most popular titles on the streaming service.

However, many fans are wondering why they can’t watch seasons 2 and 3 of Manifest on Netflix,

especially after the show was renewed for a fourth and final season by Netflix in August 2022.

Here are some possible reasons and explanations for this frustrating situation.

Netflix Doesn’t Have the Global Rights to Manifest

One of the main reasons why Netflix doesn’t have seasons 2 and 3 of Manifest in some regions is because it doesn’t have the global rights to the show.

Manifest is produced and distributed by Warner Bros. Television, which means that it can license the show to different broadcasters and platforms around the world.

In some countries, such as the United Kingdom, Manifest is exclusive to Sky and its streaming service NowTV, which has the rights to the show until at least March 31st, 20251.

In other countries, such as Canada, Manifest is available on Global TV and its streaming service StackTV2.

Netflix only has the rights to stream Manifest in certain regions, such as the United States, Australia, India, and most of Europe.

Netflix Has to Wait for Previous Deals to Expire or Buy Them Out

Another reason why Netflix doesn’t have seasons 2 and 3 of Manifest in some regions is because it has to wait for previous deals to expire or buy them out.

For example, in the Netherlands, Netflix only had season 1 of Manifest until January 1st, 2022, when it added season 2 as well.

However, season 3 is still not available on Netflix in the Netherlands, because it is still airing on RTL4, a local broadcaster.

Netflix may have to wait until RTL4 finishes airing season 3 or negotiate with them to acquire the rights sooner.

Similarly, in France, season 3 of Manifest is still airing on TF1, another local broadcaster.

Netflix may face the same challenges in other countries where local broadcasters still have the rights to season 3.

Netflix May Not Want to Confuse or Spoil Viewers

A third reason why Netflix doesn’t have seasons 2 and 3 of Manifest in some regions is because it may not want to confuse or spoil viewers.

For instance, some viewers in the United Kingdom reported that after finishing season 1 of Manifest on Netflix, the autoplay feature automatically started playing season 4 instead of season 2.

This resulted in a huge spoiler and a lot of confusion for those who were not aware that seasons 2 and 3 were missing from Netflix UK.

Netflix may have decided to avoid this problem by not adding seasons 2 and 3 until they can add all four seasons at once.

This way, viewers can watch the entire series without any gaps or interruptions.

When Will Seasons 2 and 3 of Manifest Be on Netflix?

The good news is that Netflix has renewed Manifest for a fourth and final season, which will consist of 20 episodes and will be released in two parts.

The first part of season 4 will premiere on November 4th, 2022 on Netflix worldwide.

This means that even if you can’t watch seasons 2 and 3 on Netflix right now, you will be able to watch season 4 along with everyone else.

However, if you want to catch up on seasons 2 and 3 before watching season 4, you may have to use other options, such as buying or renting them on digital platforms like Amazon Prime Video or iTunes.

Alternatively, you can hope that Netflix will add seasons 2 and 3 soon in your region or that your local broadcaster will air them before season 4 arrives.
